These are representative examples of the kind of work DotsAI is built for: private AI systems, document-heavy automation, and operational tooling where the outcome matters more than the trend language.

What these examples show

  • Where private AI outperforms generic cloud usage
  • Where workflow automation saves real operator time
  • How system design affects cost, speed, and trust

// case study 01

Chemical factory · NeoNir reporting system

Built a centralised reporting platform that brought clients, leadership, and employees into one white-labelled operating system instead of scattered reporting files and manual status chasing.

  • Outcome: one reporting hood with RBAC, in-house integrations, and an in-house AI assistant
  • Why it worked: reporting moved from fragmented document flow to one daily operating system
  • Best lesson: when teams, clients, and leaders share one source of truth, reporting stops being a drag on operations

// case study 02

Automobile resale · inventory analytics at scale

Designed analytics rails that helped a car-resale business expand from a local operating rhythm into a much larger PAN-India inventory engine with margin visibility built in.

  • Outcome: inventory scale from roughly 400 cars/month to 9000 cars/month
  • Why it worked: the analytics layer made movement, bottlenecks, and margin quality legible before the business expanded harder
  • Best lesson: scale follows visibility when inventory and pricing data are structured properly

// case study 03

Telecom ops · performance and compliance control room

Built 350+ compliance and performance-management analytics flows across promotions, peer reviews, training, improvement plans, satisfaction, and transparency inside a telecom-heavy calling operation.

  • Outcome: natural attrition brought below 25% versus an industry norm near 38%
  • Why it worked: performance became measurable, reviewable, and fair instead of hidden inside ad-hoc manager judgment
  • Best lesson: clean operating analytics can improve both throughput and trust when the workflow is people-heavy

// case study 04

Voice agents · infra and runtime design

Planned and built the provider, latency, routing, and runtime layer for agentic voice bots aimed at serious daily volume rather than demo traffic.

  • Outcome: infrastructure designed toward 8 lakh calls per day
  • Why it worked: provider choice, latency control, and failure handling were designed as first-class concerns
  • Best lesson: voice agents become real only when infra and reliability are treated like product work, not API glue

// case study 05

Document processing · faster operator audits

Structured uploaded insurance and bank-statement review so operators could move through each customer faster without losing audit quality.

  • Outcome: audit time reduced from roughly 10 minutes to 2–3 minutes per customer
  • Why it worked: extraction, validation, and review logic were designed around the actual audit sequence
  • Best lesson: document intelligence works best when paired with explicit operator flow instead of prompt-only magic
