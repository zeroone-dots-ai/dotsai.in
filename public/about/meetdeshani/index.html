// the framework

Every engagement follows D.O.T.S.

Data, Operations, Technology, Strategy — in that order. Not strategy first, then figuring out the rest. Real margin improvement starts with understanding what data exists, how operations move, and where technology can actually change the unit economics.

D
Data

What information exists? Where does it live? What shape is it in? You cannot automate chaos. We structure and surface the right data first.

O
Operations

Which workflows are repeated? Where do humans spend time on decisions a machine can make? Operations is where the cost and the opportunity live.

T
Technology

Only after D and O are mapped does the right technology become obvious. LLMs, retrieval systems, automation pipelines — each chosen to fit the problem, not the trend.

S
Strategy

The output is margin — measurable improvement in unit economics, throughput, or headcount leverage. Strategy is the outcome, not the starting point.
