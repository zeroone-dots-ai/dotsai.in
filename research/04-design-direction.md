# Design Direction

## Design objective
Keep the same hero spirit and same general theme, but make the site feel sharper, more premium, and more expensive.

This should feel like:
- an editorial technology atelier
- a founder-led AI agency with taste
- a modern Indian business brand with conviction

This should not feel like:
- a startup template
- a bento-box SaaS page
- a neon cyberpunk AI clone
- a pastel productivity tool

## Recommended direction

### Name for the direction
`Paper / Ink / Plum`

### Why this is the best fit
- It keeps continuity with the current hero.
- It already exists inside the current brand tokens.
- It feels more premium than a rainbow or heavy-gradient direction.
- It supports both enterprise trust and founder distinctiveness.

## Best-fit palette

### Primary neutrals
- Paper: `#F7F3ED`
- Bone: `#ECE5DB`
- Ink: `#171722`
- Smoke: `#6E675F`

### Brand accents
- Deep Plum: `#43305F`
- Soft Lavender: `#D7CFF0`
- Aged Gold: `#B28743`

### Utility accents
- Fog Blue: `#BFD6EC`
- Dust Mint: `#C9DED4`
- Warm Peach: `#F1C6AE`

## How to use the colors

### 80%
- Paper, bone, ink, smoke

### 15%
- Deep plum

### 5%
- Gold and the softer pastel accents

### Rule
- The pastel quartet should become signal colors, not base UI colors.
- Use them for diagrams, data points, small markers, and highlight moments.
- Do not build whole sections from them.

## Recommended gradients
- Hero glow: `radial-gradient(circle at 50% 35%, rgba(215, 207, 240, 0.55), rgba(247, 243, 237, 0) 55%)`
- Dark transition: `linear-gradient(180deg, #171722 0%, #241D33 100%)`
- Premium shimmer: `linear-gradient(135deg, #B28743 0%, #D8C39C 45%, #8F6A35 100%)`

## Typography direction

### Recommended
- Display: keep `Instrument Serif` or switch to a similarly elegant serif
- Body: upgrade from a generic-feeling sans to a cleaner grotesk if licensing and implementation allow
- Mono: keep monospace for small labels and system cues

### Practical recommendation
- Keep `Instrument Serif`
- Consider replacing `DM Sans` with `Manrope`, `General Sans`, or `Satoshi`
- Keep the mono only for labels, metrics, and tiny navigational detail

## Layout rules
- Large vertical spacing
- Fewer boxes
- Stronger type contrast
- More asymmetric composition
- One dominant idea per viewport
- More edge-to-edge scenes
- Thinner dividers, better section pacing

## Component rules

### Replace
- dense card grids
- bento arrangements
- equal-weight section blocks

### With
- pinned editorial scenes
- large proof quotes
- narrow trust rails
- split-screen sections
- oversized metrics
- image or diagram moments with generous whitespace

## Motion direction

### Use
- slow reveal
- pinned storytelling
- masked text transitions
- diagram activation on scroll
- subtle glow movement

### Avoid
- hyperactive micro-animations
- many floating objects
- overuse of parallax
- motion that makes reading harder

## Home page visual storyboard

### Hero
- Paper background
- warm lavender light bloom
- oversized serif headline
- plum italic emphasis
- compact nav with darker pill CTA

### Chapter 2
- page darkens slightly
- three proof statements animate in sequence
- dots or nodes connect as the user scrolls

### Chapter 3
- sticky service frame
- left side narrative
- right side service scene shifts by scroll progress

### Chapter 4
- light again
- stronger proof, logos, numbers, or diagrams
- this is where gold can appear very sparingly

### Chapter 5
- founder section with portrait or signature visual
- direct writing, less abstraction

### Final CTA
- high-contrast close
- one premium card or framed panel
- no clutter

## The single most important design rule
- Premium does not come from adding more effects.
- Premium comes from stronger restraint, better hierarchy, sharper contrast, and fewer things fighting for attention.
