# Post-Launch Growth Operating Rhythm

Prepared: March 26, 2026

This is the operating cadence for turning a good launch into sustained traffic growth.

## First 72 hours

- verify that Google and Bing can fetch `robots.txt` and sitemap
- inspect homepage and primary service pages in Search Console
- test live form submissions again
- verify canonicals, OG tags, and schema on production
- watch 404, 5xx, and JS error dashboards
- verify acquisition channels are attributed correctly in PostgreSQL

## First 2 weeks

- review search query data for branded vs non-branded demand
- review top landing pages and bounce behavior
- review Core Web Vitals and interaction pain on mobile
- fix weak titles, descriptions, and internal links based on early data
- publish at least 2 to 4 proof-driven supporting pages

Examples:
- case study
- implementation teardown
- founder point-of-view article
- industry-specific private AI page

## Weekly rhythm

- review:
  - clicks
  - impressions
  - CTR
  - average position
  - leads by landing page
  - source/medium quality
- identify pages with:
  - impressions but low CTR
  - clicks but weak conversion
  - strong conversion but weak ranking
- improve:
  - titles
  - intros
  - proof blocks
  - internal links
  - CTAs

## Monthly rhythm

- publish one serious commercial page upgrade or new supporting page cluster
- refresh top service pages with clearer proof and examples
- add internal links from all new content into target commercial pages
- review competitor SERPs for:
  - title patterns
  - proof styles
  - page structures
  - local modifiers
- review entity consistency across:
  - website
  - Google Business Profile
  - LinkedIn
  - company directories

## Quarterly rhythm

- prune or consolidate weak thin pages
- review site architecture and orphan pages
- review conversion funnel quality by source
- review whether new subfolders are justified
- review backlink and citation profile quality

## KPIs to track

- non-brand clicks
- non-brand impressions
- branded clicks
- leads per landing page
- lead-to-call rate
- call-to-close quality by landing page
- top converting source/medium pairs
- top converting pages
- p75 LCP / INP / CLS

## What to avoid

- constantly changing URLs
- shipping thin service pages just to cover keywords
- publishing AI-generated filler content
- splitting into subdomains too early
- treating rankings as the only metric while conversions stay weak

## Success pattern for DotsAI
The likely winning pattern is:

1. one high-conviction premium homepage
2. a small set of strong commercial service pages
3. proof-rich supporting content
4. clean measurement
5. steady monthly improvements based on real query and conversion data
